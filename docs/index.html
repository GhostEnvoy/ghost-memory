üí¨ Step 1: Conversation

Every message you send is processed. The AI doesn't automatically remember everything ‚Äî it uses a policy layer to decide what's worth saving.

üéØ Step 2: Filter (Policy Layer)

The policy layer checks each piece of information:

  • Is it important? (importance score ‚â• 0.7)
  • Is it a fact, preference, or task?
  • Should it be stored?

üî¢ Step 3: Convert to Vectors

Text is converted into "vectors" ‚Äî lists of numbers that capture the meaning of the text. This is called embedding.

Example: "coffee" and "espresso" become nearby numbers because they have similar meaning.

üíæ Step 4: Store in Qdrant

Vectors are stored in Qdrant, a vector database. It's like a smart filing cabinet that can find similar things quickly.

üîç Step 5: Retrieve

When you ask about something, your question is converted to a vector, and Qdrant finds the most similar memories ‚Äî even if you don't use the exact same words!
